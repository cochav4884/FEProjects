Arabica makes up 60% of the worlds coffee beans while Robusta makes up 40% of coffee beans in the worldwide bean exports.Arabica are the most popular beans in the world. Arabica beans are larger, sweeter, and more expensive than their prime competition, the Robusta becuase they are harder to grow, needing more water, shade, and higher altitudes than Robusta. Robusta are grown in many of the same areas as Arabica beans, Robusta beans have a lower acidity than Arabica which makes t hem less sweet. Robusta is the type often used in expresso and instant coffee. They have almost double the caffeiene content of Arabica beans and are known for a stronger and more bitter taste and smell. Catimor coffee beans are known for being somewhat sour and astrigent. Caturra coffee beans are a high yielding variety that grows on shorter plants making them easier to harvest. Geisha coffee beans are expensive due to the specific conditions required to grow and in worldwide demand. Harar (Harrar) Artisan coffee beans are said to have a fruity flavor that somewhat resembles a dry red wine. Icatu coffee beans are a hybrid of Arabica and Robusta coffee beans and are resistand to the leaf rust disease and are high yielding due to this durability. Jackson coffee bean's natural mutation resist leaf rust disease. Jamaican Blue Mountain coffe beans are known to produce a mild flavor coffee that has made them popular worldwide. Japan imports about 80%. Java coffee beans were once widely exported and earned the slang term Java for coffee and is known as a strong mildly sweet coffee. Jember coffee beans are said to be sweet with caramel, brown sugar, or maple flavor and are not easy to find. Kent coffee beans are known to make a brew described as velvety. Kona coffee beans can be bought everywhere on the Hawaiian Islands where tourists are found. Just becareful when you purchase that the label says 100% not 10%. Liberica coffee beans are unsually tolerant to heat and grow well in low altitudes and are known to be rather consistent which limits worldwide popularity. Maragatura coffee beans are a high-yielding plant, but require a lot of maintenance and are said to produce a fine cup of joe. Maragogype coffee beans are known as the "elephant coffee bean" due to growing larger than most other varieties and are highly regarded for their high-quality brews. Mocha coffee beans are oddly shaped, have a mildly chocolaty taste. Are largely lost to posterity and the innumerable variations in coffee beans and coffee drinks. Mundo Novo coffee beans are highly productive but also disease-prone, which keeps them from a higher level of cultivation. Pacamara coffee bean was tinkered with for about 30 years under the intent to leverage the recognized high-quality coffee from Maragogype and the higher yields of Pacas. Pacas coffee beans is more prone to disease damage than some other variaties and produces a coffeee generally rated good but not great. Ruiru coffee beans unfortunately produce lower quality coffee than other varieties developed by research. Sagada coffee beans, despite its age, remain difficult to find abroad. SL-28 coffee beans wins the prize for the least imaginative name and were designed to be disease resistant with a sweet taste and strong aroma. SL-34 coffee beans are diseased prone, suprising for and engineered plant, produce a high quality coffee known for their sweeet fruity flavor. Sumatra coffee beans are home to many subvarieties of beans and are known for a plethora of hight quality varieties. Tanzania Peaberry coffee bean's unique flavor is contributed by the volcanic soil they grow in and are interplanted with banana trees to shade the bean which may or may not affect bean flavor. Timor coffee beans produce about 11 million pounds a year or about 1% of worldwide production and are known for their sweetness and mild acidity. Typica coffee beans are grown in different regions, the varying climates, elevations, moisture, and soil produce the abundance of Typica varieties we have today.
